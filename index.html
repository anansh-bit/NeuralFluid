01 // PROJECT CONTEXT

The End of the Screen

Vision

This speculative design project explores Extended Reality thirty years into the future. AR glasses have been rendered obsolete by Direct Neural Overlays — digital information injected directly into the visual and somatosensory cortices via neural dust and brain-computer interfaces.


Neural Dust BCIs Visual Cortex Injection Somatosensory Layer
Paradigm Shift

By removing the physical screen, the concept of "User Interface" is fundamentally dismantled. UI is no longer a 2D bounding box overlaid onto the world — it is a volumetric, responsive fluid that obeys the laws of synthetic physics.


"Neuro-Morphic Fluidity — where the mind is the display, and reality is the canvas."

02 // CORE SPECULATION

The Skin Economy

Physical manufacturing has become prohibitively expensive and environmentally taxing. Society has embraced "Generic Minimalism" — most physical objects are 3D-printed using cheap, matte-grey, sustainable matter.

The Neural Overlay acts as an architectural "skin," painting blank canvases in real-time. A simple block chair can visually appear as rich mahogany, with its texture physically felt via haptic synesthesia.

03 // UI ARCHITECTURE

Fluid Containers &
Spatial Infographics

01
Environmental Adherence

The UI possesses "Smart Viscosity." If summoned near a flat surface like a desk, the UI pours onto it, filling the space like water. It actively flows around physical objects to maintain a dry buffer zone.

02
Spatial Infographics & Aura Data

Information exists as fluid auras surrounding objects and people. A coffee cup has its temperature and caffeine content swirling around its rim. A person's profile manifests as a translucent liquid ring orbiting their shoulders.

03
Surface Tension & Coalescence

To minimize an application, the user pinches the fluid puddle. Its surface tension increases until it condenses into a high-density, marble-like "bead" of data that orbits the user's peripheral vision.

04
Non-Euclidean Volumes

A digital folder manifests as a floating sphere of liquid mercury. When the user reaches into it, their hand submerges, and the interior volume expands infinitely, revealing a 3D spatial gallery of assets.

04 // INTERACTION STUDY

Haptic Synesthesia

Phantom Limb Interaction
The neural overlay tricks the brain into feeling physical resistance when interacting with digital fluid. Secure documents feel highly viscous and heavy — like honey. Transient notifications feel light and misty.


Synesthetic Commands
Users can map mental commands to simulated sensory experiences. Mentally "cooling" a fluid container archives its contents, while "heating" causes the UI to expand and open.


Pourable Interfaces
Sharing data does not involve "sending" a file. A user physically pushes their digital fluid puddle across a table toward a colleague. When the puddles touch, they coalesce into a unified shared workspace.

05 // SYSTEM RESPONSIVENESS

Bio-Adaptive Latency &
Reality Filtering

01
⬡
Cognitive Filter Bubbles

The system acts as a real-time reality editor. Users apply "fluid masking" — unwanted individuals or chaotic crowds are visually softened into abstract, pixelated clouds or gentle water ripples. Two people in the same room may perceive entirely different environments.

02
◉
Emotional Refraction

The UI's visual and physical properties adapt to the user's cortisol levels and heart rate. High stress results in a jagged, high-contrast "splashing" interface. A calm state produces a smooth, glassy pool with slow, rhythmic ripples.

03
◎
Gaze-Contingent Solidification

To conserve cognitive bandwidth, peripheral UI elements remain in a blurred, "unformed" liquid state. The moment the user's gaze darts toward them, they instantly snap into high-fidelity solid forms.

06 // SPECULATIVE FRICTION

Cognitive Bleed &
Digital Residue

A core requirement of speculative design is exploring unintended consequences. In 2056, the primary frictions are psychological and environmental — the blurring of digital physics with physical reality.

6.1
The Glitch-Ghetto & Digital Residue

If physical architecture is just a blank canvas, what happens when the overlay degrades? "Digital Residue" occurs when fluid UI leaves visual or haptic stains on physical surfaces — shimmering, viscous burn-ins that remain long after an app is closed.

6.2
Cognitive Bleed

When users spend a majority of their day within a Neural Fluid Overlay, the brain loses its ability to distinguish between synthetic fluid physics and real-world solid mechanics. A user might instinctively "swipe" a physical book to scroll its pages.

6.3
Diegetic Artifacts

Society has introduced physical grounding tools to combat cognitive drift — a raw sandstone Sensory Calibration Stone with zero digital connectivity, and a Reality Eraser brush to scrub away stubborn UI stains from generic furniture.

The Sensory Calibration Stone

A physical, low-tech artifact made of raw, jagged sandstone. Zero digital connectivity. When experiencing Cognitive Bleed, users rub the stone — the intense, unyielding physical texture forcibly re-anchors the brain to the rigid laws of physical matter.

The Reality Eraser

A purely physical haptic brush used to scrub away stubborn "Digital Residue" and UI stains from generic furniture, acting as a janitorial tool for the neural overlay environment.
