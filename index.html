05 // Interaction Paradigm

Haptic Synesthesia

01
Phantom Limb Interaction

The neural overlay tricks the brain into feeling physical resistance with digital fluid. Secure documents feel viscous and heavy like honey. Transient notifications feel light and misty. Weight communicates priority before text is read.

02
Synesthetic Commands

Mental states map to sensory outputs. Mentally cooling a container archives its contents; heating it opens and expands the UI. Temperature is a file operation. Thought is the gesture.

03
Pourable Interfaces

Sharing is physical. Push your data puddle across a surface toward a colleague — when the two fluid masses touch, they coalesce into a unified shared workspace with merged state and history.

07 // Speculative Friction

Cognitive Bleed
& Digital Residue

Speculative design demands interrogating unintended consequences. In 2056, the primary frictions are not hardware failures — they are psychological and perceptual, products of a brain that can no longer tell synthetic physics from real ones.

6.1 // Friction
The Glitch-Ghetto & Digital Residue

When the overlay degrades, fluid UI leaves haptic stains on physical surfaces — shimmering, viscous burn-ins that remain long after apps are closed. The desk accumulates ghostly layers of past tasks.

6.2 // Friction
Cognitive Bleed

After extended overlay use, the brain loses its ability to distinguish synthetic fluid physics from real mechanics. Users try to swipe physical books, or expect spilled water to auto-arrange into folders.

6.3 // Diegetic Grounding Artifacts
🪨 Sensory Calibration Stone

Raw, jagged sandstone. Zero digital connectivity. Rubbing its unyielding texture forcibly re-anchors the brain to the rigid laws of physical matter, suppressing the fluid overlay temporarily.

🖊 The Reality Eraser

A physical haptic brush used to scrub away Digital Residue and UI stains from generic furniture. A janitorial tool for the neural overlay — the first analogue maintenance job of the digital age.
